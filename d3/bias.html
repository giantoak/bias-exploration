Sliding adjustment of biased data

We designed this tool to give analysts a sense for the hidden variables that mediate relationships between variables in a dataset.

This particular graph allows the exploration of how the relationship between number of logged ads in a city and the logged city population is mediated by local education levels.

Generally speaking, we know that more educated people tend to live in big cities. So if we think places with more education are less likely to procure sex services online, then every sex ad in a high education place is more informative adn should be upweighted

The interpretation of an increase in slope magnitude (ie. making the slider value more negative) increasing the slope of the counts/population relationship (which appears to be happening) is that places which are more educated tend to have relatively more backpage ads per capita.
